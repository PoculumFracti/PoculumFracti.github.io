First the song selection.

Battle Hymn of the Republic. A great tune to learn. It is a very religious song but it was played and sung during battle by the North as a marching tune. The tune is deep south in origin, being transformed into a popular tune, then an abolitionist song, then a patriotic song. It was originally not well received in the South due to the association with the war.

The south had very different patriotic songs, however, most folk tunes were sung by both sides off of the battle field.

The Bonnie Blue Flag was a popular song sung by the South during the civil war. The tune is from many folk songs, such as Coleman's March, The Irish Jaunting Car, and Whistle on Your Way. The tune has some grisly origins but a mini history of the formation of the Confederate States of America was written and sung over the melody as a marching tune.
