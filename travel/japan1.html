Hotels, Ryokans, Hostels

Stays can be gotten same day often quite easily.

  • Love hotels usually require Male and Female guest but can be gotten for short stays or over night.
    • Some do not let you leave once you check in so do not automatically think you can put your stuff in and walk around town after
    • Old and stinky
    • usually cannot stay more than one night at a time.
    • Great for temporary nap, like a few hours midday
  • Booking.com and Agoda.com are great for last minute hotels. Agoda usually has cheapest price but is very inflexible if there is a problem. If you can speak Japanese it can be negotiated over phone, however, if you can you should reserve with the hotel directly because often that has the best price.
  • AZ hotel is very popular and usually very clean. they charge PER PERSON so for two people expect about 80 dollars a night.
  • DEALS! Hotels have deals for long stays. Have to go to the hotel website to find these out.
  • Hostels are plentiful. They are full of white people who will not talk to you. They are also full of non-white people who will. These come in many forms
    • BUNK > shared army barrocks style bedding = Cheapest
    • small room or double > check pictures. Some rooms are the size of one queen size mattress. Some are very large.
    • Sometimes industrial style with cement floors
    • Almost always a shared bathroom.
    • Website usually has best price, and if you are a returning guest you can get an even cheaper deal through suave negotiation tactics.

      Ryokans

      Ryokans are bed and breakfast but there is no bed and often no breakfast. The food is usually only on specific days of the week and the price of said food will be included in THAT DAY's rent. The anticipation MAY be that you DO NOT LEAVE during your stay or only leave for a little time and return for meal time and leisure time SOMETIMES WITH THE OWNER OR HELPER. Do not be alarmed, because this is usually made clear in the booking. No bed means you will sleep on a mat on the floor. Ryokan websites exist and usually offer the best prices, however, most ryokan's are small and have limited rooms so reserving ahead of time might be necessary.

Transportation

Cars drive on the Left side of the road.

  • In Tokyo people will not avoid you
  • In Kyoto they will actively walk into you
  • In Fukuoka they will stop and move off to the side and then hand you a gift as you pass Transportation is in the following types

    Walking

    By far the best and most fun

    Bicycle or Rent a scooter

    If you are staying in Kyoto for several days, consider renting a bicycle. DO NOT do this the first day you land because you will crash into people.

    Car

    You can rent a car IF you apply for an international license. This can be done easily and quickly in most USA states. I do not recommend driving your first time in a left side of the road country.

    Taxi

    You cannot call for a taxi without a Japanese phone number. You can hail a taxi. They are expensive but absolutely worth it. Remember, no tipping.

Trains

Trains are split into several categories

  • Local
  • Subway
  • Rapid - less stops
  • Limited (or special) - Requires separate ticket
  • Shinkansen - Requires separate ticket Local makes stops at every station.
    Subway is sometimes not any different at all from local and sometimes the same train will go to both above ground and subway stations making their difference very questionable.

[!NOTE] You can identify what type of train it is by the name or writing on the train itself

Trains descriptions will have:

  • Name of the LINE (rail system)
  • Name of the TERMINATING STATION
  • Identifying NUMBER
  • STYLE of train So it may appear as : Keihin-T≈çhoku Line Local Omiya 10
  • Keihin-Tonoku Line - The rail system
  • Local - Stops every station
  • Omiya - Goes to Omiya
  • 10 - Identifying number Multiple lines can go to the same station Google Maps might show the platform but always check the marquee boards at the stations.

Special Trains

Limited

Limited Express costs extra money and sometimes does not get you there faster. They are luxary rides but are sometimes the only option to get between certain cities. The cost is high but sometimes results in a nice ride. Some of them are very fast.

Shinkansen

The rail systems in Japan are mostly privately owned by JR companies. There are many. JR EAST is usually the only one most tourists will interact with unless you go West of Kyoto. Each JR company has different passes but you can get the JR Rail Pass which does 90% of trains.

You can order a Shinkansen on the smartEX ap, online, at the station kiosk, or at a service desk. NOT ALL SERVICE DESKS are for all JR. If you are at a station that services multiple JR companies you will have to go to THAT JR DESK to use their services.

Each LIMITED and each SHINKANSEN

will require a separate ticket. If you are taking a trip that involves transfers you will get a ticket for each train (unless you are using a pass)

Reserved means you pick your seat, non-Reserved means you might have to stand

Usually, non-Reserved is okay, but be aware you might have to stand or wait for the next train. Most Limited and Shinkansen only have 1 or 2 cars "Open" for "Free" or non-reserved seating. The concierge may refer to it as "open" seating. Non-reserved is cheaper but you may not have a seat. There is no charge if you have to wait for the next train.

SOME LIMITED DO NOT HAVE ANY OPEN CARS AND ARE RESERVATION ONLY
